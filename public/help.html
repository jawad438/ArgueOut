What is ArgueOut?

ArgueOut is a live video debate platform that pairs you with someone who holds opposing political views. You fill out a Political Compass to place yourself on a spectrum from left to right (economic) and authoritarian to libertarian (social). The platform then matches you with your political opposite and connects you for a face-to-face, real-time video debate.

Every debate uses a topic generated by AI — tailored to the specific combination of people in the room. There are no pre-set scripts. You argue your real position against a real person who disagrees with you.

Why debate? The best way to stress-test your beliefs is to defend them live, against someone who disagrees with everything you stand for. ArgueOut is built for that.

Creating an Account

Registration takes about two minutes and is split into two steps.

Step 1 — Identity

1
Go to /register or click Get Started on the homepage.
2
Optionally click the avatar circle to upload a profile picture (JPG/PNG/GIF, max 5 MB).
3
Enter your full name, a unique username (3–20 characters, letters/numbers/underscore), email, and a password (at least 6 characters).
4
Check the Terms of Service box and click Continue. You can also sign up instantly with your Google account.

Step 2 — Demographics

5
Fill in optional context: date of birth, gender, religion / worldview, country, and a short bio (max 280 characters). None of this is required but it helps give your opponents meaningful context.
6
Complete the security check (Cloudflare Turnstile CAPTCHA) — this appears automatically in step 2.
7
Click Create Account & Continue. You'll be taken straight to the Political Compass setup.
Age requirement: You must be 18 or older to use ArgueOut. If we discover you are under 18, your account will be terminated and your data deleted.

Setting Your Political Compass

After registration you're taken to /compass. This is the most important step — your compass position determines who you debate.

You'll be presented with a series of AI-generated questions tailored to your profile. Answer each one honestly on a scale from "Strongly Agree" to "Strongly Disagree". After completing all questions, your answers are scored and your position is placed on a 2D grid.

The Two Axes

  • Left–Right (horizontal): Economic views. Left = collective / state-managed economics. Right = free market / private enterprise.
  • Authoritarian–Libertarian (vertical): Social views. Authoritarian = strong government control. Libertarian = personal freedom and minimal state intervention.

The Four Quadrants

Auth-Left

State-led economy, strong government control. Typical views: socialism, communism, strong welfare state.

Auth-Right

Free-market economy, strong social order. Typical views: conservatism, nationalism, traditional values.

Lib-Left

Collective economics, personal freedom. Typical views: progressivism, social democracy, environmentalism.

Lib-Right

Free-market economy, social freedom. Typical views: libertarianism, classical liberalism, voluntaryism.

Once set, your compass position is visible to potential opponents in the queue and shown on your lobby profile card as a quadrant badge (e.g. "Auth-Left").

You can redo your compass at any time from the lobby. See Updating Your Compass below.

Your Profile

The left panel of the lobby is your profile card. It shows your avatar, display name, username, quadrant badge, compass coordinates, and your optional demographics (age, gender, religion, country, bio).

Changing Your Profile Picture

Click your avatar circle in the profile panel. A file picker opens — choose a JPG, PNG, or GIF (max 5 MB). The image is automatically resized and saved to your account. The change appears immediately in the lobby and will be visible to your next debate opponent.

Editing Your Display Name

Click the pencil (edit) icon next to your display name in the profile card. Type your new name and press Enter or click the save button. Usernames cannot be changed after registration.

Finding a Debate Partner

To start debating, click the large "Find Debate Partner" button in the centre of the lobby. This adds you to the matchmaking queue.

1
Click Find Debate Partner. A spinner appears and the button changes to "Leave Queue".
2
When another user joins the queue, the algorithm instantly pairs you with the most politically opposite person available.
3
A short countdown appears, then both users are redirected to the debate room automatically.

Click Leave Queue at any time before matching to exit the queue without penalty.

How Matching Works

ArgueOut uses a simple but effective algorithm: it calculates the Euclidean distance between your compass position and every other person waiting in the queue. You're paired with whoever is furthest away from you — maximum political opposition.

If you're an Auth-Left user (top-left quadrant), the algorithm looks for a Lib-Right user (bottom-right quadrant) and pairs you with the strongest match available.

If the queue is small, you may be matched with someone who isn't perfectly opposite — they're simply the most opposite person currently available.

Video & Audio

When the debate room loads, your browser will ask permission to access your camera and microphone. You must allow both to participate fully.

  • Your video appears in a small thumbnail in the bottom-right corner of the screen.
  • Your opponent's video fills the main area of the screen.
  • Video is peer-to-peer (WebRTC) — it goes directly between your browsers, never through ArgueOut's servers.
  • Noise suppression (RNNoise) is applied automatically to your microphone to filter background noise like fans, keyboards, and ambient sound.

Use the microphone button to mute / unmute yourself. Use the camera button to turn your video on or off.

For the best experience, use headphones to prevent microphone echo. A quiet room and good lighting also help your opponent take you more seriously.

The Turn System

ArgueOut uses a structured turn-based speaking system to ensure both sides get a fair chance to make their case.

  • Your Turn (green glow): You have the floor. Make your argument. Your microphone is active and your video glows green to signal you're speaking.
  • Opponent's Turn: Your opponent is speaking. Listen and prepare your counter-argument.
  • Request to Speak: If you urgently need the floor while your opponent is talking, click the "Request to Speak" button. Your opponent will see the request and can pass the turn.

The debate topic is displayed at the top of the screen throughout the debate. It's generated fresh for each pairing by the AI using both participants' profile information.

When it's your turn, get to the point quickly. Concise, well-reasoned arguments are far more effective than long rambling statements.

Chat Panel

The right side of the debate room has a live text chat. You can use it any time — even when it's not your speaking turn — to:

  • Share a specific fact, statistic, or quote
  • Clarify a position quickly without interrupting the flow
  • Drop a link to a source or article
  • React to what your opponent just said
Chat messages are not stored. They only exist for the duration of the debate session. Once either participant leaves, all chat messages are gone permanently.

Ending a Debate

Click the "Leave Debate" button (top-right of the debate room, red) to exit at any time. There is no minimum debate duration — you can leave whenever you want.

If either participant leaves, the debate ends for both. The remaining participant will see a notification and be returned to the lobby automatically.

After a debate, you're taken back to the lobby where you can immediately queue for a new match.

Editing Your Profile

Most profile fields set during registration can be updated from the lobby profile panel. Click the relevant field to edit it inline. Changes save automatically.

  • Display name — click the pencil icon next to your name
  • Profile picture — click your avatar
  • Bio — click the bio text to edit it
  • Political compass — click "Update Compass" (see below)

Email address and username cannot be changed after registration.

Multiple Accounts

ArgueOut lets you sign in to multiple accounts and switch between them without logging out and back in. Accounts are stored locally on your device — no passwords are ever saved.

Adding an Account

1
In the lobby, click your username / avatar in the top navbar. The account switcher dropdown opens.
2
Click "Add another account".
3
Sign in with Google or enter the username/email and password of the account you want to add.
4
The lobby reloads as the new account. All your accounts are now shown in the switcher.

Switching Accounts

Open the account switcher (click your username in the navbar) and click any account in the list. The active account has a checkmark. Switching prompts you to re-authenticate (enter the account's password or use Google) for security, then the page reloads.

Signing Out

Open the account switcher and click "Sign out". This signs you out of the current account only. Other stored accounts remain in the switcher for next time.

Updating Your Political Compass

Your political views may evolve, or you might feel your initial answers didn't reflect you accurately. You can redo your compass at any time:

1
In the lobby, find the "Update Compass" button in your profile panel (below your quadrant badge).
2
You'll go through a fresh AI-generated questionnaire based on your current profile.
3
Your new position replaces the old one immediately and affects all future matchmaking.

Platform Rules

ArgueOut is built for honest political discourse. Disagreement, even sharp disagreement, is welcome. The following are not:

  • Harassment, threats, or personal abuse directed at your opponent
  • Hate speech targeting race, religion, gender, nationality, sexual orientation, or other protected characteristics
  • Deliberate misrepresentation or impersonation of another person or group
  • Deliberately spreading provably false information to deceive
  • Attempting to extract personal information from opponents under false pretences
  • Using bots, scripts, or automation to interact with the platform
  • Circumventing a ban by creating a new account

These rules exist to keep debate productive. Vigorous disagreement, including on sensitive political topics, is entirely fine. Abuse is not. See the full Terms of Service for the complete rules.

Reporting a User

If your opponent is violating the platform rules during a debate, use the report button (flag icon) in the debate room controls.

1
Click the flag icon in the debate room.
2
Select the reason: Hate speech, Harassment / threats, Impersonation, or Other.
3
Confirm the report. An admin will review it. You can continue or leave the debate after reporting.
Don't report someone simply because they're winning the debate or hold views you find offensive. Report only genuine rule violations. False reports may result in action against your own account.

Bans & Enforcement

If you violate the Terms of Service, an admin may suspend or permanently ban your account. Banned users see a ban screen when they try to log in, which explains the reason for the ban.

  • Bans are reviewed on a case-by-case basis by admins.
  • ArgueOut does not have an appeals process at this time.
  • Bans are permanent unless reversed by an admin.
  • Creating a new account to evade a ban is a violation of the Terms and will result in both accounts being banned.
IP bans: In severe cases, bans may be applied at the IP level, preventing access even from new accounts on the same network.

Camera & Microphone Problems

If your camera or microphone isn't working in the debate room, try these steps:

1
Look for a camera / microphone permission prompt from your browser. If you accidentally denied it, click the padlock icon in the address bar and set Camera and Microphone to "Allow", then refresh.
2
Close any other application that may be using your camera: Zoom, Teams, Skype, OBS, FaceTime, etc. Only one app can use the camera at a time on most devices.
3
Check your system privacy settings. On Windows, go to Settings → Privacy → Camera / Microphone and make sure your browser is allowed. On Mac, go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera / Microphone.
4
Try a different browser. Chrome and Firefox have the best WebRTC support. Safari works on iOS 15.4+.

Connection Issues

ArgueOut uses peer-to-peer WebRTC for video. This means your video stream travels directly from your browser to your opponent's browser, without passing through any server. If you can't see or hear your opponent:

  • Check your internet connection — try loading another website or video.
  • Refresh the page — connection issues often self-resolve on reconnect.
  • If you're on a corporate or university network, it may block WebRTC traffic. Try a mobile hotspot or a different network.
  • If one side sees the other but not vice versa, the issue is on the side that can't see — usually a firewall or browser setting blocking incoming WebRTC.
  • Disable any VPN — some VPN configurations prevent direct peer connections.

Clearing the Cache / Page Looks Outdated

ArgueOut uses a service worker to cache assets for fast loading. In rare cases the cache can serve a stale version of a page that's missing recent updates.

To force a full refresh:

  • Windows/Linux: Press Ctrl + Shift + R
  • Mac: Press ⌘ + Shift + R
  • Or open DevTools (F12) → Application tab → Service Workers → click Unregister → refresh the page.

This tells the browser to bypass all cached files and fetch fresh versions from the server.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ArgueOut free to use?
Yes, completely free. There are no paid tiers, no subscriptions, and no ads. ArgueOut may introduce optional features in the future with advance notice, but the core platform will remain free.
Are debates recorded or stored?
No. Video and audio use peer-to-peer WebRTC and are never routed through ArgueOut's servers. Chat messages are not stored either — they exist only for the duration of the session. Once a debate ends, all of it is gone.
Can I choose my own debate topic?
No. Topics are generated fresh by an AI for each specific pairing, using both participants' profile information and compass positions. This creates more personalised and relevant debates than a fixed topic list would.
Can I pick who I debate?
No. The matching algorithm automatically selects your most politically opposite opponent from the queue. This ensures every debate is a genuine clash of ideas rather than a friendly discussion between people who already agree.
Why was I matched with someone who has similar views?
The algorithm picks the most opposite person currently in the queue. If the queue is small (especially at off-peak times), your match may not be perfectly opposite — they're simply the best available option. Try queuing at different times for more varied matches.
Do I need to have my camera on?
No, you can participate without a camera. Your video thumbnail will appear blank to your opponent. However, face-to-face video makes debates more engaging and harder to dismiss — having your camera on is strongly encouraged.
What browsers does ArgueOut support?
Any modern browser with WebRTC support: Google Chrome (recommended), Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Safari (iOS 15.4+ / macOS). Internet Explorer is not supported. For best results use the latest version of Chrome or Firefox on desktop.
Is there a mobile app?
There is no native iOS or Android app. ArgueOut is a web app designed to work in mobile browsers. You can add it to your home screen from your browser menu for an app-like experience (it includes a web manifest for this).
Can I delete my account?
Account self-deletion is not currently available. If you want your account and data removed, please contact the platform owner via the website. Deletion requests are handled at the admin's discretion. See the Privacy Policy for more detail.
Why is my compass result not what I expected?
Compass positions are calculated from your answers. If your result feels inaccurate, try answering more carefully — "Agree" means you lean that way, "Strongly Agree" means it's a firm conviction. You can redo the questionnaire anytime via Update Compass in the lobby.
What happens if my opponent leaves mid-debate?
The debate ends immediately for both participants. You'll see a notification and be returned to the lobby automatically. There's no penalty for either party — you're free to queue again right away.
Is my email address visible to other users?
No. Your email is used only for account management (authentication and recovery). It is never shown to other users and is not stored in your public profile. See the Privacy Policy for a full list of what information is and isn't shared.
I set my demographics (age, religion, etc.) — does my opponent see all of that?
Yes — demographic information you provide (gender, religion, country, bio) is shown to your debate opponent as context. This is intentional: knowing who you're arguing against makes the debate more meaningful. Information you leave blank or set to "Prefer not to say" is not shown.
How do I contact the ArgueOut team?
ArgueOut is an independently run platform. There is no public support email at this time. For urgent issues (abuse, data requests, ban appeals), use the feedback or contact mechanism available within the platform, or reach out through any public channel the platform owner has listed.
