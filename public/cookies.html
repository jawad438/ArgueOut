Cookies & Local Storage

Last updated: July 1, 2026

This page explains what ArgueOut stores on your device and why, in plain language. It supplements the Privacy Policy. Whenever we say "cookies" below we also mean similar browser storage technologies ArgueOut actually uses, like localStorage.

Essential

Required for the app to function. These can't be turned off — without them you can't stay signed in or connect to a debate.

NamePurpose
Firebase Auth sessionKeeps you signed in between visits.
Socket.io connectionThe live connection your browser uses to reach the matchmaking/debate/chat server. Not persisted after you close the tab.
ao-consent-v1Remembers your cookie preference choices themselves, so we don't ask again every visit.
ao-anon-idA random ID (no personal data) used only to attach a consent choice to a compliance record if you're not signed in.
userIdUsed by the interface to know whether to show "Sign In" or your logged-in navigation.

Functional

Optional. These remember preferences so the app behaves the way you left it. You can turn these off — the app still works, it just won't remember these choices next time.

NamePurpose
ao-themeYour dark/light/system theme choice.
debateRoomId / debateOpponent / debateQuestionLets you rejoin an in-progress debate correctly if the page reloads.
avatarDataUrlA cached copy of your profile picture so it loads instantly.
Saved account listLets you switch between multiple ArgueOut accounts on the same device without re-entering credentials.

Analytics

ArgueOut does not run any analytics today — no Google Analytics, no Firebase Analytics, no usage tracking of any kind. This category exists in the preference center so that if we ever add privacy-respecting analytics in the future, it will already be off by default and require your explicit opt-in — nothing changes for you unless you turn it on.

Third-Party

ArgueOut runs no advertising or third-party tracking scripts. Two third-party services are used to operate core features, disclosed here for transparency even though neither is a tracker in your browser:

  • Google Firebase — authentication and profile storage. Required for the app to work (Essential).
  • OpenRouter — an AI API called from our server (not your browser) to generate debate topic questions from your profile attributes. No cookie is set in your browser by this call.

See the Privacy Policy for full detail on both. This "Third-Party" toggle is reserved for any future genuinely third-party tracking/ad script — turning it off changes nothing about Firebase or OpenRouter, since those are essential-path services, not optional trackers.

Do Not Track & Global Privacy Control

If your browser sends a Do Not Track or Global Privacy Control signal, ArgueOut detects it and defaults the Analytics and Third-Party toggles to off when the preference center first opens. You can still explicitly turn them on if you want.

Re-consent

Your saved preference is remembered for up to 12 months, and is automatically re-requested sooner if we materially change this cookie policy (tracked by a version number attached to your saved choice).
