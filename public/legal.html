Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how ArgueOut collects, uses, and protects your personal information. By creating an account you acknowledge this policy and agree to its terms.

1. Information We Collect

When you register for an account, ArgueOut collects and stores the following information:

  • Display name
  • Username
  • Email address
  • Age (number)
  • Gender
  • Religion or worldview
  • Country
  • Bio
  • Profile picture (optional)
  • Political compass position (set by you via the onboarding questionnaire)
  • Account creation timestamp
  • Whether your political compass has been set

We also transiently read your IP address during registration and login solely to auto-detect your country. This IP address is not stored.

2. Information We Do Not Store

  • Video call footage — debates use peer-to-peer WebRTC; no video or audio passes through our servers
  • Chat text messages exchanged during debates
  • IP addresses (used only transiently for country detection)
  • Browser type, device identifiers, or navigation behaviour

3. How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is used solely to operate ArgueOut. Specifically:

  • To create and manage your account
  • To display your public profile to potential debate opponents
  • To match you with compatible debate opponents using your profile attributes
  • To generate personalised debate topics via an AI system
  • To enforce platform rules including age limits and bans

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, or any purpose beyond operating the platform.

4. Third-Party Services

ArgueOut uses a limited set of third-party services to operate. These are:

  • Google Firebase — handles user authentication and stores your profile data in Firestore. Firebase is operated by Google LLC and subject to Google's privacy policy.
  • OpenRouter — an AI API used to generate debate topic questions. Your profile attributes (e.g. political position, religion, country) may be sent to OpenRouter to generate personalised questions. OpenRouter does not receive your name or email.
  • ip-api.com — used once during registration to auto-detect your country from your IP address. Your IP is not stored by ArgueOut after this lookup.

No other third-party services that collect user data are used. There are no advertising networks, payment processors, or analytics tools.

5. Audio Processing

ArgueOut applies client-side noise suppression (RNNoise) to your microphone audio during debates. This processing happens entirely within your browser. No audio is sent to ArgueOut servers at any point.

6. Data Sharing

ArgueOut does not sell or share your personal data with any third parties beyond the services listed in Section 4. Profile information that is publicly visible on the platform (username, bio, political position, country, etc.) is visible to other ArgueOut users by design.

7. Data Retention and Account Deletion

Your profile data is retained for as long as your account exists. Users cannot self-delete their accounts. The only circumstances under which your data may be removed are:

  • Your account is banned for violating the Terms of Service
  • You were found to be under 18 at the time of registration
